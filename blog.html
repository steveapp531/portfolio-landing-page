The Privacy Policy You Never Read—But Definitely Should!

Written By: Stephen Appiah

Do you always read the privacy policy before signing up for a website? Or is it just too long and confusing to bother with? You're not alone! Most people scroll right past it without a second thought. But here’s the thing—your personal information matters, and you have the right to know how it’s being used. That’s exactly why understanding privacy policies is so important. They tell you what data a company collects, why they need it, and how they protect it (or don’t). But let’s be real—most of them are packed with complicated legal terms that make your head spin. In this post, we’re breaking it down in simple terms so you can take control of your online privacy without the headache. Let’s dive in!

#1. What is Privacy Policy?

A privacy policy is a statement or legal document (in privacy law) that discloses some or all of the ways a party gathers, uses, discloses, and manages a customer or client's data. Elisa, et al. (2012)
Personal information can be anything that can be used to identify an individual, not limited to the person's name, address, date of birth, marital status, contact information, ID issue, and expiry date, financial records, credit information, medical history, where one travels, and intentions to acquire goods and services.
In the case of a business, it is often a statement that declares a party's policy on how it collects, stores, and releases personal information it collects. It informs the client what specific information is collected, and whether it is kept confidential, shared with partners, or sold to other firms or enterprises.
Privacy policies typically represent a broader, more generalized treatment, as opposed to data use statements, which tend to be more detailed and specific.
